Dry vs Wet Anthrax Example:
Some Observations
• Several kinds of knowledge need to be acquired:
– BW Process models
• “To produce dry Anthrax, the spores are fermented, then milled and
dried…”
– Evaluation criteria to assess those process models
• “Dry anthrax is more difficult to produce in terms of product
stabilization, spore size, and worker safety…”
• Note that the evaluation criteria are expressed in terms of
the information in the process models
– Fermentation makes the spores grow, and obtaining certain spore
sizes may require specific fermentation techniques.  The
techniques needed for the dry Anthrax spore sizes have more
requirements than those needed for wet Anthrax sizes, thus they
are more difficult.
