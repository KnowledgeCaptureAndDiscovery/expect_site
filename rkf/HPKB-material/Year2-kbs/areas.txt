constant: ControlMeasure.

in Mt: ModernMilitaryTacticsMt.

isa: CombatTheaterSiteAttribute IKBConstant.

sourceOfTerm-PIT: FM101-5-1Mt.

sourceOfTerm-WebPage: FM101-5-1WebPage.

comment: "Instances of #$SpatialThing-Localized which are accorded

this attribute in a particular COA development scenario gain the

status ofcoordination features which aid in that particular COA

development process.  Instances are used in order to to assign

responsibilities, coordinate fires and maneuver, and control combat

operations. Each control measure can be portrayed graphically. In

general, all control measures should be easily identifiable on the

ground. Examples of control measures include boundaries, objectives,

coordinating points, contact point, and direction of attack. From FM

101-5-1.".

cyclistNotes: "Since practically any terrain feature or human

construct can be a 'control measure' in the right circumstances, most

types of #$ControlMeasure are not represented using collections: the

concept does not in any sense delineate a natural kind.  Instances of

certain kinds of things: e.g., instances of #$PhaseLine, will always

be control measures, but many other things: hills, valleys, telephone

poles, office buildings, and so forth, will only have the status of

'control measure' within a particular #$COASpecificationMicrotheory.".

special Course of Action context represented by COAMT, SPATIALTHING

has the special status of 'Control Measure'.  This means that

SPATIALTHING is a coordination feature which aids in the development

process of that particular COA.  Instances are used in order to to

assign responsibilities, coordinate fires and maneuver, and control

combat operations. Each control measure can be portrayed

graphically.  In general, all control measures should be easily

identifiable on the ground. Examples of control measures include

boundaries, objectives, coordinating points, contact point, and

direction of attack. From FM 101-5-1.".

cyclistNotes: "Since practically any terrain feature or human

construct can be a 'control measure' in the right circumstances, most

types of #$ControlMeasure are not represented using collections: the

concept does not in any sense delineate a natural kind.".

;F:

;(implies
