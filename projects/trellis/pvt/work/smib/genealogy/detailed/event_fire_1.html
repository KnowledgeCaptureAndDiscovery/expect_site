Event: Stolpce Fire

When: July 1915

Supported by:

  • Stolpce Yizkor Book states that a fire destroyed the town in July 1915.

  • History of the Baltic 1914 leads me to believe that the Russians, in retreat from a German offensive in the Spring of 1914, burned the town to deprive the Germans of a strategic outpost, situated as it was on the Neman river and the Warsaw-Minsk railway line.
