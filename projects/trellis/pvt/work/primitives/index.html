Discourse Relations

  • A provides background for B
  • A is elaborated in B set:members, abstract:instances, whole:part, process:steps, object:attribute, graliz:spec
  • A is solved by B
  • A shows how to do B
  • A is motivation for B
  • A depends on B
  • A otherwise B
  • A causes B

    A causes choice of B

    A resulted in B

  • A results in B

    Choosing A resulted in B

    A happened and resulted in B

  • A is purpose of B
  • A stands though contradicted by B (= B dismissed given A)
  • A conceding B
  • A can be interpreted through B
  • A evaluated by B
  • A restates B
  • A summarizes B
  • A in contrast with B
  • A . B (no relation)

Logic Connectives

  • Not A
  • A and B
  • A or B but not both
  • A or B or both
  • A therefore B
  • If A therefore B then Not B therefore Not A
  • If A and A therefore B then B
  • If Not A and A or B but not both then B
  • If Not B and A therefore B then Not A
